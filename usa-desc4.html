University of Michigan Scholarships

The University of Michigan is a top-ranked public university with a history of excellence in research, teaching and learning, sports, and arts. The university offers scholarships to students who want to pursue their undergraduate and graduate studies in any of the 240-degree programs.

Gloria Wille Bell and Carlos R. Bell Scholarship Program

Offered to first-year students with an outstanding academic record, who are residents of an eligible area and pursuing an undergraduate degree in science in the College of Literature, Science, and the Arts or in the College of Engineering. The annual renewal of this scholarship depends on satisfactory academic progress.

  • Value: $20,000 per year for non-resident students, $10,000 per year for resident students.

Sidney J. and Irene Shipman Scholarship

Dedicated to incoming University of Michigan students with excellent academic talent, leadership skills, and talent in art and science. Residentsâ€™ students who receive the full shipman scholarship are awarded $20,000 for four years, while students that come from other states are awarded $30,000 per year.

  • Value: $20,000 for residents, $30,000 for non-residents.

Penelope W. and E. Roe Stamps Award

Awarded to students that demonstrate exceptional skills, leadership qualities, academic achievement, and a commitment to their community. Stamps Scholars receive a renewable scholarship for four years of undergraduate study and the value of the scholarship can go up to the full cost of attendance.

  • Value: Full cost of attendance.

Additional Merit Scholarships

The College of Literature, Science, and the Arts (LSA) award about 40 additional merit-based scholarships to qualified first LSA students. To be considered for these awards, applicants must complete the four-Year Renewable Scholarship application by the deadline.

General Eligibility Criteria

Requirements for Freshmen:

  • An international student with a valid visa.
  • GPA of 3.0 (4.0 scale).
  • SAT Cumulative score of 1020 or higher (ACT composite score of 20).
  • Full-time degree-seeking enrollment at University of Michigan-Dearborn.
  • Renewable with a GPA of 3.0 at UM-Dearborn.
  • Proficiency in English score of IELTS (6.5), TOEFL (IBT-80, CBT-213, PBT-550), or MELAB (76).

Transfer Requirements for International Students at University of Michigan-Dearborn

  • Valid visa required for international students to avoid any legal issues
  • GPA of 3.5 (4.0 scale) is mandatory to ensure academic excellence
  • Full-time degree-seeking enrollment at University of Michigan-Dearborn is required to fully experience campus life
  • GPA of 3.0 at UM-Dearborn required for renewal, so keep up the good work
  • Proficiency in English demonstrated through IELTS (6.5), TOEFL (IBT-80, CBT-213, PBT-550), or MELAB (76) required to enhance communication skills

How to Apply for Michigan Scholarships in the United States

Ready to apply for a scholarship? The University of Michigan's official website is your go-to. Applicants must complete an online application form and submit required documents. Please note that application deadlines vary by program, so keep an eye out!
